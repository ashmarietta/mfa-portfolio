title: WHO I AM
---
Hello, my name is Allison Marietta, and I want to be an author when I grow up!

I am a fiction and young adult writer pursuing my Master of Fine Arts in Creative Writing at The New School. I graduated summa cum laude from Saint Louis University in three years with degrees in Research Intensive English and Spanish and learned a lot about discipline and collaboration from balancing rigorous academics with Division I athletics. 

I learned even more following graduation, this time about myself, when I took a gap year to explore the styles of writing that felt most fulfilling to me outside of the academic setting and gain new perspectives as a world traveler, assistant cross country coach, and substitute teacher. These experiences developed my leadership, interpersonal communication, and understanding of the stories and experiences that connect people. 

My published work includes 10 articles in Her Campus Magazine and two literary publications in The Kiln Project and VIA Literary Magazine, where I also served as an editor. Drawing from my experiences as an athlete, educator, reader, and traveler, I write fiction that explores identity, purpose, ambition, and human connection. I value hard work, consistency, and storytelling that feels both intimate and universal.

When I am not typing on my laptop with a candle burning in the background, I am training for a half marathon, trying a new coffee shop with my twin sister, reading on my porch, or baking something (hopefully) delicious.
